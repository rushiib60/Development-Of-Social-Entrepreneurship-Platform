# Development of Social Entrepreneurship Platform

> **Smart India Hackathon 2018 — Grand Finale** | Ministry of Skill Development & Entrepreneurship | Problem Code: **#MSD8**

A connective web platform that bridges the gap between the Government of India and social entrepreneurs — enabling ministries to outsource problem statements and startups to discover, develop, and deliver innovative social solutions.

**Team Leader:** Rushikesh Borse | **AICTE Application No:** 1-3321509102

---

## Problem Overview

Despite numerous government initiatives, there is currently **no dedicated platform** that caters exclusively to the Social Sector. Ministries want to outsource their problems to startups to get innovative solutions from across India, while entrepreneurs lack a single hub to discover and pursue meaningful social projects.

---

## Features

### 1. Uploading Problem Statements
- Ministries upload verified problems in a prescribed format
- Ministry officials post challenges at both technical and management levels
- District Collectors can upload district-level problems
- Citizens can write blogs on social topics to inspire entrepreneur-driven problem statements
- Citizen complaints are reviewed and verified by the respective ministry

### 2. Searching Social Problems
- Problems sorted by priority: **Central → State → Union Territories → District**
- Search by social topic or blog category
- Location-based search to find problems in a specific vicinity

### 3. Problem Solution Submissions
- Upload solutions via presentations, pitch videos, and documents
- Solutions ranked by startup ratings for ministry visibility
- All submissions are **timestamped** to preserve intellectual property
- Entrepreneurs can seek advice from domain experts

### 4. Evaluation & Verification by Ministry
- Technical evaluation by the concerned ministry
- Ministry contacts the team if clarifications are needed
- Official permission granted before development begins

### 5. Project Development
- E-meetings for negotiations between ministry and entrepreneurs
- Project estimation module
- Apply for **CSR funds** via integrated forms
- Entrepreneurs can pitch to private investors on the platform
- Regular status updates and frequent E-meetings between all parties
- Final project delivery via demonstration videos and reports
- Ministry can make solutions public on the platform

### 6. Impact Assessment
- Both qualitative and monetary impact assessment
- Guidelines follow the **Council for Social Development** framework

## Additional Features

### Profile System
| Role | Profile Contents |
|------|-----------------|
| **Entrepreneur** | Skills, experience, projects, endorsements, patents |
| **Expert** | Domain specialization, projects evaluated |
| **Investor** | Projects funded, topics of interest |
| **Ministry Official** | Reviews, problem statements submitted |
| **Startup** | Ratings, scope, domain, current & past projects, solutions submitted |

### Collaborative Environment
- Startups in the same domain can search and connect with each other
- Entrepreneurs can work as freelancers for startups needing specific skills
- Government officials and startups can collaborate through a shared online channel

### Rating System (SIA — Social Impact Assessment Module)
- Overall startup rating based on reviews and project ratings
- Rating privilege: Ministry > Expert > Investor > Public likes
- Ratings guide government and private investors in funding decisions

### Informative Window
- Display projects under NIF and CSIR
- RFP uploads by ministries for various projects
- CSR opportunities posted by the government CSR department

## Scope & Impact

- Reduces dependence on the global market for social projects
